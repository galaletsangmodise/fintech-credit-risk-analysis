Credit Risk Analysis Findings 
Objective 
The goal of this analysis was to identify patterns in borrower behaviour and loan performance in order to understand which factors increase
the likelihood of loan default. 

Key Findings 
Overall Portfolio Risk The loan portfolio shows a 50% default rate, indicating a high-risk lending environment. 
Credit Grade Risk Borrowers with Grade C loans represent the highest number of defaults. This suggests that the credit grading model 
may underestimate the risk associated with this borrower segment. 
Income Risk Borrowers with lower annual income levels appear more likely to default compared to higher income borrowers. 
Loan Purpose Risk Loans issued for personal purposes show the highest number of defaults compared to other loan categories. 

Strategic Implications
A fintech lender could reduce portfolio risk by: 
- tightening approval criteria for mid-risk credit grades
- introducing minimum income thresholds
- limiting loan amounts for higher-risk purposes
- strengthening credit risk models 

Conclusion This analysis demonstrates how SQL can be used to identify credit risk patterns and support data-driven lending 
strategies in fintech environments.
